DESIGN NOTES

Geometrical Properties of Common Shapes

1. Rectangle

Area: \( A = b \times h \)

Centroid: \( \bar{x} = \frac{b}{2} \)

Centroid: \( \bar{y} = \frac{h}{2} \)

2. Circle

Area: \( A = \pi r^2 \)

Centroid: At center

3. Triangle

Area: \( A = \frac{1}{2} b h \)

Centroid: \( \bar{x} = \frac{b}{3} \)

Centroid: \( \bar{y} = \frac{h}{3} \)

4. Semi-Circle

Area: \( A = \frac{1}{2}\pi r^2 \)

Centroid: \( \bar{x} = 0 \)

Centroid: \( \bar{y} = \frac{4R}{3\pi} \)

5. Quarter-Circle

Area: \( A = \frac{1}{4} \pi r^2 \)

Centroid: \( \bar{x} = \frac{4r}{3\pi} \)

Centroid: \( \bar{y} = \frac{4r}{3\pi} \)

6. Trapezium

Area: \( A = \frac{1}{2} (a + b) h\)

Centroid: \( \bar{x} = \frac{a^2 + b^2+ab}{3(a+b)} \)

Centroid: \( \bar{y} = \frac{h(2a+b)}{3(a+b)} \)

How to Determine Centroid Location

The centroid of a shape represents its geometric center. For simple shapes:

  • Symmetrical shapes: Centroid is at the intersection of axes of symmetry
  • Composite shapes: Use the weighted average of component centroids
  • Irregular shapes: Divide into simple shapes and use \( \bar{x} = \frac{\sum A_i x_i}{\sum A_i} \), \( \bar{y} = \frac{\sum A_i y_i}{\sum A_i} \)
