PREPARATION OF INTERACTION DIAGRAM AS PER BS 8110: 1997

Input parameters.

Concrete grade \(f_{cu}\)
Steel grade \(f_{y}\)
Modulus of Elasticity \(E_s\)
Effective cover \(d'\)
Axial force \(N\)
Moment at x-axis \(M_x\)
Moment at y-axis \(M_y\)
Shorter dimension \(b\)
Longer dimension \(h\)
Effective width \(b'=b-d'\)
Effective depth \(h'=h-d'\)
Percentage of reinforcement \(p\)
No. of rows \(n_r\)
Number of reinforcement \(n\)
Spacing of reinforcement \(S_b\)

The following conditions shall be fulfilled to prepare the interaction diagram.

i) When neutral axis lies in the section.

Fig.: Stress-strain diagram of column when neutral axis lies in the section \(0.9x≤h\).

Computation of strains

Strain of compression bars

\(\varepsilon_{sc1}=\frac{x-d'}{x}\varepsilon_{cu} \)

And

\(\varepsilon_{sc2}=\frac{x-d'-S_b}{x}\varepsilon_{cu} \)

And so on........depending upon the number of rows of rebar where \(\varepsilon_{cu} = 0.0035\)

Strain at tensile reinforcement

\(\varepsilon_{st}=\frac{h'-x}{x}\varepsilon_{cu} \)

Computation of Stresses

At compression rebar

\(f_{sc1}=\varepsilon_{sc1}E_s\)

If \(f_{sc1}\geq 0.87f_y\), then \(f_{sc1}=0.87f_y\) Else \(f_{sc1}=\varepsilon_{sc1}E_s\)

And

\(f_{sc2}=\varepsilon_{sc2}E_s\)

If \(f_{sc2}\geq 0.87f_y\), then \(f_{sc2}=0.87f_y\) Else \(f_{sc2}=\varepsilon_{sc2}E_s\)

And so on........depending upon the number of rows of rebar.

At tension rebar

\(f_{st}=\varepsilon_{st}E_s\)

If \(f_{st}\geq 0.87f_y \) then \(f_{st}=0.87f_y\) Else \(f_{st}=\varepsilon_{st}E_s\)

Computation of forces

Of Concrete:

\(F_{cc}=0.45f_{cu} * 0.9x *b \)

and therefore

\(F_{cc}=0.405f_{cu}bx \)

Of Compression rebar

\(F_{sc1}=A_{sc1}(f_{sc1}-0.45f_{cu})\)

And

\(F_{sc2}=A_{sc2}(f_{sc2}-0.45f_{cu})\)

And so on……depending on the number of rows of rebar

Of tension rebars

\(F_{st}=A_{st}(f_{st}-0.45f_{cu})\)

Resultant forces in concrete

\(N=F_{sc1}+F_{sc2}+F_{cc}-F_{st}\)

Calculation of moment capacities (Taking moment at center of the section).

Of Compression rebar

\(M_1=F_{sc1}(\frac{h}{2}-d')\)

And

\(M_2=F_{sc2}(\frac{h}{2}-d'-S_b)\)

Of Concrete

\(M_c=F_{cc}(\frac{h}{2}-\frac{S}{2})\)

Where \(s=0.9x\)

Of Tension rebar

\(M_s=F_{st}(h'-\frac{h}{2})\)
\(M=M_1+M_2+M_c+M_s \)

ii) When neutral axis lies outside the section

Fig.: Stress-strain diagram of column when neutral axis lies outside the section \(0.9x>h\).

Computation of Strains

Row-1

/(\varepsilon_{sc1}=\frac{x-d'}{x}\varepsilon_{cu} \)

Row-2

/(\varepsilon_{sc2}=\frac{x-d'-S_b}{x}\varepsilon_{cu} \)

And so on......

Note: all the rebars are on compression side.

Computation of Stresses

Row-1

\(f_{sc1}=\varepsilon_{sc1}E_s\)

But not greater than \(0.87f_y\).

Row-2

\(f_{sc2}=\varepsilon_{sc2}E_s\)

But not greater than \(0.87f_y\).

And so on......

Compression forces

Row-1

\(F_{st1}=(f_{sc1}-0.45f_{cu})A_{sc1}\)

Row-2

\(F_{st2}=(f_{sc2}-0.45f_{cu})A_{sc2}\)

And so on......

Compressive stress of Concrete

\(F_{cc}=0.45f_{cu}bh\)

Total force at the section

\(N=F_{sc1}+F_{sc2}+F_{sc3}+F_{cc} \)

Moment at the center of the section

\(M_1=F_{sc1}(\frac{h}{2}-d')\)
